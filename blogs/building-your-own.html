On to building your own instead of just using one

I want to talk about CH3SH1RE.

Not because it's impressive right now. Honestly it's not. It can search the web, hold a conversation, and that's about it. But that's not really the point.

I started building it because I got tired of the feeling that every AI tool I used knew more about me than I was comfortable with. Every query logged, every conversation stored somewhere on a server I have no visibility into. I'm not paranoid about it, I just don't love it. And being the kind of person who would rather build a solution than complain about a problem, I started CH3SH1RE.

The name is a nod to the Cheshire Cat. Something that appears and disappears, leaves an impression, and doesn't overstay its welcome. That's the kind of AI I wanted. One that works for me, on my terms, with my data staying where I can see it.

Right now it's pretty humble. Web search, basic responses, nothing groundbreaking. But I'm planning to give it vision so it can see and interpret what I'm looking at. Code integration so it can actually help me build things rather than just talk about them. And better reasoning, because half the time current AI tools give you a confident answer that falls apart the moment you push back on it.

I don't know if CH3SH1RE will ever be anything more than a personal project. But it's mine, and I understand every line of it, and that means something to me.

If you've ever felt like the tools you use own you a little bit, I get it. Build your own. It's worth it.
